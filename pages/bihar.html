🌟 Highlights

What Makes Bihar Special

🌿

Bodh Gaya

The most sacred Buddhist site in the world — where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree. The Mahabodhi Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site visited by millions every year.

📚

Nalanda University

Ruins of the ancient Nalanda Mahavihara, the world's first residential university dating back to the 5th century AD. A UNESCO World Heritage Site that once drew scholars from across Asia.

🏰

Rajgir

Ancient city surrounded by five hills, associated with both Buddhism and Jainism. Visit the Gridhrakuta hill, Vishwa Shanti Stupa, and the ancient Cyclopean Wall.

🛕

Vaishali & Pataliputra

Vaishali was the world's first republic. Pataliputra (modern Patna) was the Mauryan Empire's grand capital. Rich with Ashoka Pillars and archaeological wonders.

🎨

Madhubani Art

Bihar's famous folk art tradition — intricate geometric patterns and nature motifs painted on walls and paper. A vibrant cultural heritage practiced by local women for centuries.

🪔

Chhath Puja

Bihar's grandest festival — a deeply spiritual celebration dedicated to the Sun God. Witnessed on the banks of the Ganga, it is one of India's most visually stunning cultural events.
